About Gene Hackman

Gene Hackman is an acclaimed American actor and author. He was born on January 30, 1930, in San Bernardino, California. Hackman is known for his versatile acting skills and has received numerous accolades, including two Academy Awards, four Golden Globe Awards, and a Screen Actors Guild Award.

His breakout role came in the 1967 film "Bonnie and Clyde," where he portrayed the character of Buck Barrow. Throughout his career, he starred in various iconic films, including "The French Connection," "The Conversation," and "Unforgiven."

After a successful acting career spanning several decades, Hackman retired from acting in 2004. He passed away on February 26, 2025 at the age of 95

Academy Award Wins and Nominations

  • Best Actor (Won) - The French Connection (1971)
  • Best Supporing Actor (Won) - Unforgiven (1992)
  • Best Supporting Actor (Nominated) - Bonnie and Clyde (1967)
  • Best Supporting Actor (Nominated) - I Never Sang for My Father (1970)
  • Best Actor (Nominated) - Mississippi Burning (1988)
